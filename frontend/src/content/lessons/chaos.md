## stable
### instruction
Drag the blue r down to about 2.8. Watch the time series settle to a single value.

### hint
Drag r in the formula down to 2.80.

### insight
At r=2.8, the system settles to a fixed point. This is like a stable ecosystem -- the population finds its equilibrium and stays there.

## oscillation
### instruction
Now drag r up to about 3.2. Watch the time series start to oscillate between two values.

### hint
Increase r to about 3.20.

### insight
The system now bounces between two values -- a period-2 cycle. Like a population that overshoots, then undershoots, then overshoots again. Predictable, but no longer a fixed point.

## chaos
### instruction
Push r all the way up to about 3.8. Welcome to chaos.

### hint
Drag r up to 3.80.

### insight
The time series now looks random, but it's not -- it follows a deterministic equation. This is chaos: perfectly predictable rules producing apparently unpredictable behavior.

## butterfly
### instruction
Now unlock: change x₀ by just 0.01. Even a tiny change in initial conditions leads to a completely different trajectory.

### hint
Drag the amber x₀ slightly up or down.

### insight
This is the butterfly effect. In a chaotic system, a butterfly flapping its wings in Brazil can change the weather in Texas. That's why weather forecasts fail after about 10 days -- tiny measurement errors grow exponentially.
