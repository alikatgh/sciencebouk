## explore-real
### instruction
Drag the blue a (the real part) to move the point left and right along the real axis.

### hint
Find the blue a in the formula and drag it.

### insight
The real part moves the point horizontally, just like a regular number line. Complex numbers extend math into two dimensions.

## explore-imaginary
### instruction
Now drag the yellow b (the imaginary part) to move the point up and down.

### hint
Drag the yellow b value in the formula.

### insight
The imaginary axis is perpendicular to the real axis. Together they form the complex plane, where every point represents a complex number a + bi.

## multiply-by-i
### instruction
Set a to about 1 and b to 0, then use the "Multiply by i" button below the visualization. Watch what happens to the point.

### hint
Set a=1, b=0 to put the point on the real axis, then click the multiply-by-i button.

### insight
Multiplying by i rotates the point exactly 90 degrees counterclockwise. Do it twice (i x i) and you've rotated 180 degrees, which is the same as multiplying by -1. That's why i squared equals -1!
